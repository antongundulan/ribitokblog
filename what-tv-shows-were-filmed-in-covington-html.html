Covington, Georgia: Hollywood's Playground in the South (Hold My Sweet Tea!)

Ever wondered where your favorite shows filmed those charming small-town scenes? Buckle up, because we're taking a hilarious trip to Covington, Georgia, a place that's basically a revolving door for Hollywood!

From Dukes to Diaries: Covington's Hall of Fame

This quaint town south of Atlanta has become a filming hotspot, and for good reason. Covington's historic buildings and scenic squares transform into backdrops for all sorts of stories. We're talking everything from classic tearjerkers to supernatural teen dramas.

  • Did someone say "Hazzard County"? Yep, that's right! The iconic jumps of the General Lee were filmed on the streets of Covington – talk about a legendary entrance.
  • Calling all Vampire Diaries fans! Mystic Falls, anyone? The Salvatore Boarding House, the Mystic Grill – these spooky (and sometimes steamy) locations all come courtesy of Covington's magic touch.
  • Law and Order: Covington Branch? Believe it or not, the gritty police drama "In the Heat of the Night" used Covington to portray its fictional Mississippi town.

This is just a taste, folks! From "My Cousin Vinny" to "The Originals," Covington's resume is longer than a Southern drawl.

Covington: More Than Just a Pretty Facade

Sure, Covington looks good on camera, but there's more to this town than meets the eye (or the lens).

  • Feeling like a star? Take a walking tour and see the filming locations for yourself! You might even snag a selfie with a Mystic Falls ghost (don't worry, they're friendly).
  • History buffs not left out! Covington boasts a rich history, and you can explore it at the Covington Historical Society Museum.
  • Foodies, rejoice! Covington's got some down-home Southern cooking that'll make your taste buds do a jig.

So, next time you're watching your favorite show and a charming Southern town pops up, there's a good chance it's Covington.

Covington Calling: How You Can Be Part of the Fun!

How to Spot a Filming Location: Keep your eyes peeled for those telltale signs – closed streets, trailers parked around town, and maybe even a glimpse of a costumed extra.

How to Take a Filming Location Tour: The Covington Visitor Center offers fantastic tours that take you to all the iconic spots.

How to NOT Disrupt a Filming Crew: Remember, these folks are working! Be respectful, keep your distance, and avoid photobombing that dramatic scene.

How to Eat Like You're on Set: Head to The Mystic Grill (yes, it's a real restaurant!) or any of Covington's other delicious Southern eateries.

How to Channel Your Inner Mystic Falls Resident: Pack your best flannel shirt and practice your brooding stares. You never know, you might just blend right in!
