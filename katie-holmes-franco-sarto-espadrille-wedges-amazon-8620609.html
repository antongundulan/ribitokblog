Katie Holmes Just Wore a Pair of Under-$45 Espadrille Wedges

 Posted on August 14, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  383 words  |  Kelle Repass

Over the years, Katie Holmes has established herself as a trendsetter in budget-friendly footwear. She’s been spotted wearing comfy Reebok sneakers, ‘It’ girl Adidas Sambas, and versatile Birdies ballet flats while out and about in New York City. Most recently, Holmes stepped out in Franco Sarto espadrille wedges that are perfect for spring, and you can snag a pair of the shoes on sale at Amazon from $43. 

While Holmes’ exact beige and black colorway is going for full price, other variations of the shoes, including a neutral woven style and bright pink leather version, are discounted. The espadrilles are designed with 3-inch, jute-wrapped wedges, padded insoles, a thick strap around the toes, and an ankle strap with an adjustable hook-and-loop closure, so you can find your most comfortable fit. 

These elevated sandals come with a host of styling opportunities. Take a note from Holmes and wear them with cropped trousers and a knit top for a chic daytime look; jazz them up for a night out with a mini dress and a matching clutch; or, pair them with a floral midi dress for a garden-themed spring wedding. 

While Holmes may be the most famous fan of the Franco Sarto espadrilles, more than 1,200 Amazon shoppers have given them a five-star rating, too. One person called the shoes “comfortable and sturdy,” adding that the “straps around the ankle feel very secure,” and the “soles are made of rubber,” which “prevents any slippage.” A second reviewer confirmed there was “no breaking-in period,” while someone else said they experienced “no rubbing or blisters” even on the first wear.

Other shoppers raved about the versatility of the sandals. A reviewer said they’re “awesome for summer events,” since you can style them with “dresses, jeans, [or] capris.” Plus, a second shopper said the heels are nice enough to “dress up an outfit,” but they “could still run errands in them and be comfortable.” That same person went so far as to say, “If you're on the fence about these shoes, don’t be — buy them.”
