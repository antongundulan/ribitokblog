Why is Chicago called O Hare airport?

 Posted on August 4, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  682 words  |  Billy Koelling

Chicago is known for its bustling airport, but have you ever wondered why it is called O’Hare airport? The name actually pays homage to a heroic figure in American aviation history – Lieutenant Commander Edward “Butch” O’Hare. O’Hare was a World War II flying ace and the first U.S. Navy recipient of the Medal of Honor. Let’s delve into the fascinating story behind the airport’s name.

During World War II, O’Hare served as a Navy pilot and became famous for his heroic actions in defending the USS Lexington from a wave of Japanese bombers. On February 20, 1942, Butch O’Hare interrupted a formation of nine enemy bombers that were approaching the aircraft carrier. Displaying incredible courage and skill, he shot down five bombers and damaged a sixth, effectively preventing any harm to the ship and her crew. For his extraordinary bravery and exceptional flying skills, O’Hare was awarded the Medal of Honor by President Franklin D. Roosevelt.

In 1949, Chicago’s Orchard Depot Airport was renamed O’Hare International Airport in honor of Lieutenant Commander Edward “Butch” O’Hare. The decision was made to commemorate his courageous actions and contributions to the nation. Today, O’Hare airport stands as a significant reminder of the bravery and dedication of Lieutenant Commander O’Hare, serving as a gateway for millions of travelers each year.

Frequently Asked Questions about O’Hare Airport

1. Who is Edward “Butch” O’Hare?

Edward “Butch” O’Hare was a World War II flying ace and the first U.S. Navy recipient of the Medal of Honor. He is known for his heroic actions in defending the USS Lexington from enemy bombers.

2. How did O’Hare airport get its name?

O’Hare airport was named after Lieutenant Commander Edward “Butch” O’Hare to commemorate his brave actions and contributions to the United States during World War II.

3. What is the significance of the name O’Hare for Chicago?

The name O’Hare holds great significance for Chicago as it represents the bravery and dedication of Lieutenant Commander O’Hare, a national hero who defended the USS Lexington from enemy bombers.

O’Hare airport handles millions of passengers each year. In 2019, it was ranked as one of the busiest airports in the world, facilitating over 83 million passengers.

O’Hare airport serves as a hub for multiple major airlines, including American Airlines, United Airlines, Delta Air Lines, and many others. It offers a wide range of national and international flights.

O’Hare airport has a total of eight runways, enabling it to efficiently handle the high volume of air traffic and ensure smooth operations for incoming and departing flights.

8. What is the airport code for O’Hare airport?

The airport code for O’Hare airport is ORD, which is derived from the previous name of the airport, Orchard Depot.

9. Does O’Hare airport have direct flights to international destinations?

Yes, O’Hare airport offers direct flights to numerous international destinations across the globe. It serves as a major international hub, connecting travelers to various countries and continents.

11. How can I get from O’Hare airport to downtown Chicago?

Travelers can reach downtown Chicago from O’Hare airport through various transportation options, including taxi services, ride-sharing apps, airport shuttles, and public transportation such as the Chicago Transit Authority (CTA) trains and buses.

O’Hare airport plays a significant role in boosting the local economy. It generates employment opportunities, attracts tourism, and drives business activities in the surrounding areas, making it an essential economic asset for Chicago.
