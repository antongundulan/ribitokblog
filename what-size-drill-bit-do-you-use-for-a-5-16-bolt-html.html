What’s more, how much larger should a hole be than the diameter of a bolt?

When drilling a tight fit hole, use a drill bit that is 1/32 inch bigger than the bolt diameter, and when drilling a loose fit hole, use a drill bit that is 1/16 inch larger than the bolt diameter.

Aside from the information provided above, how can I determine what size drill bit to use?

Hold the shaft of the drill bit up in front of the screw to prevent it from slipping. The threads of the screw should be the only thing that can be seen (and maybe a bit of the screw). It’s possible that the drill bit is too large if the threads aren’t visible. In this case, the screw is too large to be seen through the hole in the bit.

Drilling Holes Before Drilling: The primary advantage of pre-drilling holes for deck screws, or any screws, is that it prevents the wood from splitting during the screwing process. What’s the difference between a tapered and a straight drill bit, and how do you use them?

The screw threads contact a straight bit pilot hole over their entire length, creating a tight fit. Second, when the screw goes near the bottom of the tapered pilot hole, the greater thread contact provided by the tapered hole provides for better thread engagement. As the hole becomes smaller, the threads penetrate deeper into the wood.

What is the size of pilot holes?

Generally speaking, the pilot hole should be at least as big as the screw’s minor diameter when working with hardwoods. Depending on whether the screw has deep threads or if the wood is very hard, the pilot hole should be an additional 1/64-in. bigger in diameter than the minor diameter. The pilot hole might be as small as 1/64-inch in diameter for softer woods.
