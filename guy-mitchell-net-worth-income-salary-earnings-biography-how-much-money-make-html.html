A.G. Cernik, better known as Guy Mitchell, was an American pop singer who had success in his own country, as well as the United Kingdom and Australia. He was born on February 22, 1927, and died on July 1, 199During his time as a global recording sensation in the 1950s, he earned record sales in excess of 44 million copies, with six million-selling singles among his accomplishments. In the autumn of 1957, Mitchell launched his own ABC variety programme, The Guy Mitchell Show, which ran for two seasons. Also in 1961, he had a recurring part as George Romack on the NBC western detective series Whispering Smith, which starred World War II hero Audie Murphy.
